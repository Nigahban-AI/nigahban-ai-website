## Your Cameras Are Already Watching

Pakistan has an estimated 5 million CCTV cameras installed across commercial buildings, factories, warehouses, and markets. These cameras capture thousands of hours of footage every day. Yet the vast majority of that footage serves only one purpose: evidence after something has already gone wrong.

What if those same cameras could actively protect the people inside those buildings — without a single piece of new hardware?

## How AI Video Analysis Works

At its core, AI fire detection is a form of computer vision — the same technology that allows smartphones to recognize faces or self-driving cars to identify pedestrians. Here is how it works at a high level:

**1. Continuous Frame Analysis**
The AI system connects to your existing CCTV camera feeds and analyzes each video frame in real time. It examines visual patterns across the entire frame, looking for the telltale signatures of fire and smoke — flickering light patterns, rising plume shapes, color gradients associated with combustion, and rapid changes in scene composition.

**2. Dual-Layer Verification**
A single detection is not enough to trigger an alert. Nigahban AI uses a **proprietary dual-layer AI verification system** that cross-checks every initial detection through a second, independent analysis layer. This two-stage approach dramatically reduces false positives. A cooking flame, a lit cigarette, or sunlight reflecting off glass will not trigger a false alarm — but a genuine fire will be caught within seconds.

**3. Confidence Assessment**
The system assigns a confidence score to each detection. Only events that pass both verification layers and exceed the confidence threshold trigger an alert. This means building managers receive alerts they can trust, not a constant stream of false alarms that erode confidence in the system.

## Instant Multi-Channel Alerts

When a verified fire event is detected, the system dispatches alerts simultaneously through multiple channels:

- **WhatsApp**: Instant message to designated building managers and security personnel — the platform everyone in Pakistan already uses.
- **Email**: Detailed alert with timestamp, camera location, and confidence level sent to management teams.
- **Telegram**: Real-time notifications for operations teams and emergency coordinators.

Alerts reach the right people in **under 3 seconds** from detection — faster than any human monitor could react, and orders of magnitude faster than traditional smoke detectors.
